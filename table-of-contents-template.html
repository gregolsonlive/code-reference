Members’ health and wellness

Helping your employees and their dependents make healthy choices is important. Members’ health is our priority. We offer a wide range of programs, including our clinical management programs, that empower members to be healthy and stay well.

The goals and benefits of our Member Health and Wellness programs are to:

  • Encourage preventive healthcare to mitigate future healthcare challenges and costs.
  • Support chronic disease management to improve quality of life.
  • Provide catastrophic case management for severe, traumatic injury, or life-threatening conditions to reduce long-term health risks and costs.
  • Educate members about care navigation to help them find high-quality, affordable care that meets their needs.

We believe in a personal approach to healthcare. Our Member Health and Wellness team gets to know the members they work with to ensure they receive access to care and services when they need it. The programs provide:

  • A personal touch to the administration of healthcare benefits.
  • Clinical information and health education to members to inform healthcare decision-making.
  • Members with skills for prevention, treatment and recovery processes related to their needs.

More information about our Member health and wellness programs is available on our website at the Capital Blue Cross website.

Health and wellness programs

Many employers realize the best solution to rising healthcare costs is by encouraging their employees to stay healthy. We offer a comprehensive, proactive, and systematic approach to managing employee health that is effective for employers, and appealing to and confidential for employees.

Our comprehensive program is made up of tested and proven efforts and are designed to deliver a better return on your employee heath investment.

Our Health and Wellness programs enable employers to:

  • Help employees improve their health.
  • Help healthy members reduce their risk of future illness.
  • Provide support system of consulting, content, and resources.
  • Help your staff operate more efficiently and effectively.

Based on the results of your employees’ health assessments, we make recommendations on health and wellness programs that are available to your employees based on their unique health needs. Evaluation tools help to assess the effectiveness of our health and wellness programs.

Our comprehensive approach to health and wellness addresses the health needs of all your employees, including at risk individuals and those who suffer from chronic and acute illness. Intervention programs, services, and activities are matched and made available to employees according to their needs.

Our population health identification and stratification methodology empowers the clinical team with the ability to prioritize members in their populations who are BOTH clinically at-risk AND have impactable opportunities that can be acted upon immediately to make a difference in their quality of care. Our methodology also assigns a specific category (i.e., Medical (M), Drug (R), Social (S), and Behavioral Health (BH)) to the members so that our clinical team may identify the resource most appropriate for the intervention. The programs are divided into four basic categories:

  • Wellness.
  • Lifestyle & Behavior Modification.
  • Disease/Condition Management.
  • Care Management.

Healthy Blue Rewards

Capital recognizes that each customer has a unique point of view when it comes to wellness-related programs. Our wellness solution, Healthy Blue Rewards, offers a robust, comprehensive set of engagement services.

Healthy Blue Rewards presents users with their own unique experience, guiding them on their journey to the “next right thing to do” for their health and well-being. Resources include:

  • 10 self-guided, digital coaching programs.
  • Healthy behavior tracking tools.
  • Health library and symptom checker.
  • NCQA-certified health assessment (available in English and Spanish).
  • Personal challenges.
  • Personal wellness report.

Employees can access and complete the online health assessment through the portal and app. Upon completion, participants receive a personal wellness report that includes quantitative data and personalized health recommendations. It contains a measure of overall risk and a LifeScore derived using the Whole Life Algorithm. This algorithm measures the effect of unhealthy habits over a lifetime and helps members understand the increase in life expectancy they may gain by making the recommended changes listed on the report. Each report also provides Preventive Health Services Task Force recommendations, a bio-measures section, lifestyle improvement recommendations, and a call-to-action section.

With a completed health assessment and LifeScore, the platform also delivers a highly personalized experience that intuitively guides participants on the “next right thing to do” for their health in order to drive user adoption, create a meaningful member experience, and support positive steps toward improved health.

  • 27 self-guided, digital coaching programs.

With Healthy Blue Rewards Premier, we can display an employer’s logo on the online platform and mobile application, as well as customize marketing tiles on the main dashboard page so the programs and services that are most important to their organization are front and center to their employees. These tiles also can be refreshed throughout the year in support of strategic goals.

Care Management

The care management program is committed to proactively identifying and engaging members who are at high risk for or have complex healthcare needs to implement interventions to improve outcomes.

Care management is a voluntary program that offers members:

  • Goal-oriented, individualized support.
  • Ongoing assessment.
  • Planning and monitoring.
  • Advocacy through connections with providers, other programs and community resources.

The care management program serves chronic and catastrophic populations by delivering quality care through intervention and collaboration with a focus on promoting health by:

  • Prioritizing needs.
  • Providing education.
  • Actively coordinating care to prevent readmission.
  • Assessment of Social Determinants of Health (SDoH).

The care manager works collaboratively with the member’s healthcare team, member and family/caregiver to complete the case management process and develop a member-centric plan of care. They continue to interact with and coordinate care with this team throughout the duration of the case. Attempts are made to match identified needs with existing resources, including community-based and government-supported agencies and programs.

Program offerings:

Complex care management offerings include:

  • Complex and catastrophic case management.
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
  • Heart failure.
  • High-risk maternity management.
  • Oncology.
  • Transplant.

Capital Blue Cross’ Care management program is integrated with key clinical, quality improvement, service and administrative processes within our organizational structure. These services are primarily performed through telephonic communications and mailings. The care manager works with the member, authorized representative, provider(s), and interdisciplinary team to address the needs of the member and work toward completing the plan of care.

Our care management program supports national membership and licensure. Services are provided by a registered nurse and/or social worker. Specialty services are provided across all regions to ensure clinical expertise and quality service for all participants enrolled.

Utilization management programs

Concurrent review

Concurrent review is done to evaluate and monitor the medical necessity and appropriateness of ongoing care and services. Our clinicians use standardized clinical criteria and medical policy to perform concurrent review. The concurrent review program includes concurrent review, transition of care and readmission risk assessments, and discharge planning.

Concurrent review is performed for hospital and facility inpatient admissions. Clinical review with the facility’s utilization review/quality improvement department is conducted. Transition of care and readmission risk assessment is completed on all inpatient admissions and again on discharge. The concurrent review team works in collaboration with the hospital discharge planning team to facilitate a smooth transition from one level of care to another.

Concurrent review is initiated for the following services:

  • Inpatient admissions to an acute care hospital, long-term acute care hospital (LTACH), skilled nursing facility, or acute rehabilitation hospital.
  • Selected ongoing outpatient services that require submission of a treatment plan.

When we are notified, concurrent review will be conducted within 24 hours of an emergency admission or the next business day following receipt of a treatment plan. A Capital Blue Cross nurse will review the member’s medical records or treatment plan to evaluate the continued medical necessity and appropriateness of treatment and the patient’s response to the treatment.

Follow-up reviews will be performed periodically to evaluate the medical necessity and appropriateness of continued hospitalization and/or ongoing outpatient services.

Transition of care program

Our Transition of Care (TOC) Program is a care outreach program for members transitioning to home from an inpatient facility. Our staff provide outreach calls to members within two business days of discharge to ensure a safe transition to home from an inpatient facility and will provide weekly calls for a thirty-day transition period.

Our TOC program supports members with a focus on medication reconciliation, health literacy, appointment schedule, ensuring all gaps in care have been addressed and will screen for resources or additional based on identified social determinants of health.

Discharge planning

Discharge planning is an important step of the concurrent review process. It is performed by the concurrent review nurses who coordinate the member’s discharge from a facility, arrange for alternative services, and discuss treatment options with the member or member’s physician, as appropriate.

Discharge planning looks to promote the use of appropriate outpatient follow-up services and attempts to prevent avoidable complications and/or readmissions following inpatient treatment.

Our concurrent review nurses assess areas such as:

  • The member’s living arrangement prior to hospitalization.
  • Expected living arrangements after discharge.
  • Psychosocial/financial status.
  • Assessment of local delivery system.
  • Benefit coverage.
  • Need for special medication, equipment, and other ancillary supports.
  • Additional care needs.

Preauthorization

The purpose of our medical preauthorization program is to help members receive:

  • Maximum available benefits, resources, and coverage.
  • Benefit review of medically-appropriate treatment to meet members’ individual needs.
  • Care by providers delivered in an efficient and effective manner.

Preauthorization determines member eligibility at the time of the request, benefit coverage, and the medical necessity of the proposed service.

  • Our preauthorization process involves the evaluation of requests for service before delivery of care.
  • Physicians work with our specially trained clinicians to facilitate the coordination and provision of medically-necessary healthcare services in a timely and efficient manner. .
  • We use nationally-recognized medical necessity criteria and medical policy to make medical necessity and appropriateness determinations to promote appropriate use of services.
  • In-network providers complete the preauthorization process for members. .
  • Out-of-network providers may contact us for preauthorization; however, when a member accesses an out-of-network provider, the member is ultimately responsible for obtaining a required preauthorization. .
  • When a service or procedure is performed by an out-of-network provider without obtaining preauthorization, the service or procedure is reviewed retrospectively for medical necessity.
  • When in doubt, call the Member Services number on the back of the ID card to validate preauthorization requirements because the majority of our health products have a “preauthorization penalty” (i.e., the member may have to pay a penalty if a service requiring preauthorization is not preauthorized).
  • HMO medical care is coordinated through the member’s PCP. An in-network provider must be used. For HMO members, preauthorization for out-of-network care is a necessity. If the service is not preauthorized, the claim is not paid and the member is financially responsible for the service.
  • Select outpatient services require the provider to submit a treatment plan to us for evaluation of medical necessity and appropriateness. A preauthorization nurse evaluates the request and treatment plan to determine medical necessity and appropriateness of continued care. Care may be authorized for a specific number of units (i.e., visits) before additional subsequent review is required.
  • To obtain preauthorization, providers may access our online preauthorization application on our provider portal. Formulary for prescription drugs

Our formulary preface contains important information about our Rx products. The formulary is a continually updated list of medications that represents the current judgment of physicians and pharmacists in the treatment of disease and preservation of health. Medications in all therapeutic categories are reviewed based on safety, effectiveness, and cost by our Pharmacy & Therapeutics (P & T) Committee. The formulary is a key reference tool for physicians when prescribing drug therapies for their patients.

How to find formulary information:

  • Visit the Capital Blue Cross website.
  • Log in to your secure group portal.
  • Go to Find.
  • Select Drugs to go to the Prescription drug benefits.
  • Select the formulary link for the Group Coverage from Your Employer column.

Our Rx products and formularies are designed to recognize four categories (or tiers) of prescription medications:

  • Generic Preferred (tier one/GP) and Generic Nonpreferred (tier two/GNP): Drugs containing the same active ingredient(s) as their corresponding brand name drug and approved by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for therapeutic equivalency to their brand name product. (Please note not all strengths and formulations of generic drugs have the same tier status.).
  • Brand Preferred (tier three/BP): Drugs that have been reviewed by Capital Blue Cross’ P & T Committee and found to have therapeutic advantage or overall value over nonpreferred brands, factoring safety, effectiveness, and cost..
  • Brand Nonpreferred (tier four/BNP): Drugs that have been reviewed by Capital Blue Cross’ P & T Committee and found not to have significant therapeutic advantage or overall value over alternative generic, preferred brands, or over-the-counter medications..

(Note: Not all plans include a two-tier generic benefit with a different copayment/coinsurance for each generic tier. For plans that do not have a two-tier generic benefit, the generic copayment/coinsurance will be applied to generic preferred and generic nonpreferred drugs.)

For most Rx product designs, members’ copayment or coinsurance amounts differ depending on whether a drug is generic, preferred brand, or nonpreferred brand.

Prior Authorization for Prescription Drugs

Rx products include a prior authorization requirement for certain types of prescription drugs. These drugs require prior authorization before members can obtain them as a covered benefit.

The purpose of prior authorization is to:

  • Encourage the use of clinically appropriate and cost-effective medications.
  • Avoid misuse or abuse of prescription drugs.

Drugs requiring prior authorization are identified in our formulary by Prior Authorization Required (PAR), noted after the drug name. Examples of types of prescriptions requiring prior authorization include, but are not limited to, topical acne products and growth hormones.

The prescribing physician or pharmacist must provide supporting clinical information to our pharmacy benefit manager, Prime Therapeutics, in order for the drug to be considered for coverage. Medications requiring prior authorization will not be covered if prior authorization is not obtained before filling the medication.

Step therapy

When many different drugs are available for treating the same medical condition, a process called step therapy is used to support and encourage the most appropriate drug treatment.

Our Rx products are designed so first line medications must be tried for some conditions before allowing coverage for second line medications. Drugs requiring step therapy are identified in the Capital Blue Cross formulary by ST. For example, a first line osteoporosis medication should be used before prescribing a second line brand osteoporosis medication.

For certain medical conditions, the prescribing physician or pharmacist can obtain authorization by contacting the Prime Therapeutics Prior Authorization Department.

Drug quantity management for prescription drugs

Some prescription drugs may be subject to drug quantity management under your group Rx product, meaning a specific quantity of medication is authorized to be dispensed over a specific duration or per prescription. These drugs are designated by Quantity Level Limit (QLL) in the Capital Blue Cross formulary. For example, the standard quantity limit for medication may be six (6) tabs per thirty (30) days.

Generic substitution policy for prescription drugs

Your group’s Rx product contains one of the following policies regarding generic drugs substitutions for brand prescription medication. Check your group contract to identify which of the following policies applies to your group coverage:

Generic drug substitution policies

Mandatory generic substitution

If the prescribing physician indicates “Dispense as Written” (DAW) for a brand name drug, or if the member requests a brand name drug when a generic is available, the member is responsible for the cost difference (also known as ancillary charge) between the brand name medication and the generic medication in addition to the member’s applicable brand copayment/coinsurance, up to the original cost of the brand name medication.

Restricted generic substitution

If a member requests a name brand product when a generic is available, the member is responsible for the cost difference between the brand name medication and its generic cost in addition to the member’s applicable brand copayment/coinsurance, up to the original cost of the brand name medication.

If the prescribing physician indicates “DAW,” then the member pays the applicable copayment for the brand product and is not required to pay the additional ancillary charge.

Utilization review program for prescription drugs

The purpose of Drug Utilization Review (DUR) is to identify potential drug interactions, therapeutic duplications, and other safety concerns.

As part of your Rx product, each member’s complete prescription claim profile is captured—regardless of how many in-network pharmacies the member uses.

The DUR program helps to:

  • Protect the patient from an adverse event.
  • Optimize prescribing and dispensing.
  • Improve quality of care for the patient.

The DUR program provides a back-up for important drug and member-specific pharmaceutical care concerns. The review includes interaction with the patient, pharmacist, and physician, if necessary, to ensure the patient receives the most appropriate medication while managing costs.
