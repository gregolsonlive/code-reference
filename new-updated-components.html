Filing a medical claim

In-network providers file claim forms for members.

Out-of-network providers may or may not file claim forms for members. In many cases, members will need to file their own claim forms to be reimbursed for eligible services performed by out-of-network providers.

Send medical program claim forms (including for HMO claims) to:

Medical Claims ProcessingPO Box 211457Eagan, MN 55121-3057

Members with claims questions may call the number on the back of their ID card (TTY: 711).

Claim form tips:

  • File claims promptly. While members can submit claims up to 12 months from the date of service, it is best to submit claims soon after a service.
  • Complete all information on the claim form.
  • Complete a separate claim form for each family member who receives medical services.
  • Attach all itemized bills for the services received by the individual family member to the claim form. (A separate claim form is not needed for each bill.)
  • Attach all EOB forms showing prior payment (for example, from Medicare or other insurance carriers) for bills being submitted to the claim form. (See the “Other party liability” part of this section for more information about individuals having more than one applicable coverage.)

Claims for out-of-area and traveling employees

Some businesses have employees who live and work outside the central Pennsylvania and Lehigh Valley geographic areas. In addition, members traveling outside our 21-county service area may need medical services.

While we do require a company to be headquartered in this area and have an employee base here, coverage is available to our members wherever they are.

Here’s an overview of how claims work for employees who live outside of our service area or who are traveling outside of our service area.

The BlueCard® program

The BlueCard program enables your out-of-area employees to access in-network providers using all Blue plans. Within the BlueCard program, claims processing is an integrated system among all the Blue plans.

Call 800.810.BLUE (800.810.2583) to locate an in-network provider or visit the BCBS National Doctor and Hospital Finder online.

BlueCard overview

  • BlueCard allows our members to receive the same benefits regardless of where they receive services. Their benefit program goes wherever they go.
  • A Capital member may receive covered services at the in-network rate from a facility or professional provider outside our service area if the provider is a member provider with the local Blue plan. The member only needs to show their ID card and pay any applicable out-of-pocket amounts.
  • The ID card information allows the provider to file the claim correctly. The provider files the claim with the local Blue plan, which electronically routes the information to us for processing.
  • We respond to the initiating local Blue plan after applying your group’s applicable benefits and the local Blue plan pays its network provider for the covered service.
  • HMO members must reside within Capital’s service area. The BlueCard program applies for urgent and emergent care only. Nonemergency (or routine) care must be coordinated by the HMO member’s PCP.

Services from out-of-network providers

In certain situations, it might not be possible to reach a BlueCard in-network provider. Your out-of-area resident employee might also choose to receive services from an out-of-network provider.

In addition, HMO members do not have coverage for services from an out-of-network provider.

Members may be required to submit a claim for the services received from an out-of-network provider. Find claim forms on our Employer Forms page.

International travel

Blue Cross Blue Shield Global® Core provides our members with access to network healthcare services around the world. Members traveling or residing outside the United States have access to doctors and hospitals in more than 200 countries.

HMO members must reside within our service area or participate in the Away From Home Care—Guest Membership Program. HMO members may use Blue Cross Blue Shield Global Core for urgent or emergent care only.

How to locate providers outside the U.S.:

  • Phone: 800.810.BLUE (800.810.2583), 24/7
  • Web: Visit Global Core, accept the “Terms & Conditions,” enter your three-digit ID card prefix, and log in.

Tips for members who may require international services:

  • Members should carry their ID card when traveling.
  • For emergency care, members should go to the nearest hospital and call the Blue Cross Blue Shield Global Core Service Center at 800.810.BLUE (800.810.2583) or call collect at 804.673.1177, 24/7, if admitted.
  • For nonemergency care, members should call 800.810.BLUE (800.810.2583). A medical coordinator, in conjunction with a medical professional, will assist the member in finding appropriate care. The Blue Cross Blue Shield Global Core Service Center is staffed with multilingual representatives and is available 24/7. HMO members should only call Blue Cross Blue Shield Global Core for urgent or emergent care. Nonemergency (or routine) care must be coordinated by the HMO member’s PCP.
  • If preauthorization is required, members should call us at 800.471.2242.

Prescription drug claims

Capital Blue Cross’ prescription drug benefits are administered by Prime Therapeutics1, one of the largest pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs) in the nation.

Our prescription drug benefit typically has three primary components:

  • Retail service.
  • Home delivery.
  • Specialty drugs through Accredo Health Group, Inc.2

Prescription drug benefits also may have copayment, coinsurance, deductible, and/or ancillary charge requirements for prescription drugs. Cost-sharing amounts may differ depending on whether the dispensed drug is part of one of these tiers:

  • Generic preferred (GP) and generic nonpreferred (GNP) – Includes drugs that contain the same active ingredient(s) as their corresponding brand name drug and have been approved by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for therapeutic equivalency to their brand name product. Note that not all strengths and formulations of generic drugs have the same tier status.
  • Brand preferred (BP) – Includes drugs reviewed by our Pharmacy and Therapeutics Committee and found to have therapeutic advantage or overall value over nonpreferred brands, factoring safety, effectiveness, and cost.
  • Brand nonpreferred (BNP) – Includes drugs reviewed by our Pharmacy and Therapeutics Committee and found not to have significant therapeutic advantage or overall value over alternative generics, preferred brands, or over-the-counter drugs.

Note: Not all plans include a two-tier generic benefit with a different copayment/coinsurance for each generic tier. For plans that do not have a two-tier generic benefit, the generic copayment/coinsurance will be applied to both generic preferred and generic nonpreferred drugs.

Services requiring special information

Certain types of services may require additional information on the provider’s bill. In most cases, providers are aware of the types of information needed to process insurance claims, and the information they provide to the member will contain the appropriate information.

Here’s an overview of specific information that must be included on bills for certain services:

Service received

Information needed

Surgery (for example, appendectomy, cholecystectomy, hysterectomy)

Specific type of surgery.

Anesthesia

The length of time the patient was under anesthesia and the specific type of surgery for which the anesthesia was given.

Home healthcare

The type of service (for example, physical medicine, occupational therapy, speech language pathology, skilled nursing, home health aide) and date(s) of service.

Blood

The number of pints received, the charge for each pint received, and the number of pints replaced by donor(s).

Chemotherapy

The name of the drug, its dosage, the charge for each drug, and the method of administration (for example, oral, intramuscular injection, intravenous).

Home medical equipment

A certification from the prescribing provider indicating medical necessity and the expected length of time the equipment will be needed. If the bill is for rental equipment, the purchase price of the equipment must be included.

Ambulance information

The point of origin and destination (for example, from hospital to skilled nursing facility). If mileage is charged, the number of miles must be included.

Other billing arrangements

Self-funded arrangements

Some self-funded groups pay a monthly administrative fee for every enrolled subscriber or member, depending on their financial arrangements. The group also pays for the claims paid by Capital Blue Cross for the arrangement’s specific billing period.

We invoice self-funded groups differently from the groups that pay a monthly rate. As long as the groups are HIPAA compliant, we also send a list of claims we have paid on the group’s behalf.

Health savings account (HSA) and health reimbursement arrangement (HRA) billing

Separate vendors bill and collect the fees for the administration of HSA/HRA accounts. Groups must contract directly with a banking partner for HSA services.

Specific billing practices

Actual effective date/split month billing

In actual effective date/split month billing, the coverage or cancellation effective date is the date you provide. We enroll initial and newly eligible members or terminate members effective as of the date you choose. We approve that date according to our retroactive policy.

Additions

If the effective date of the new enrollee(s) falls within the first 15 days of the billing cycle, we apply a charge. If the effective date falls after the 15th day but before the first day of the next billing cycle, we don’t apply a charge.

Terminations

If the effective date of termination falls within the first 14 days of the billing cycle, we apply a credit. If the effective date of the termination falls after the 14th day but before the first day of the next billing cycle, we apply a charge.

Our systems use through logic instead of to-date logic. For example, if you terminate a member effective July 11, the member’s coverage is effective through the end of that day — 11:59 PM on July 11. The termination is effective at midnight on July 12.

Newborn or adopted dependents

Always report the addition of a newborn or adopted child, even if there’s no rate change. If the contract is not for family coverage until the birth of the newborn, your group must pay for family coverage after the billing period for the month the child was born if enrollment was submitted within 60 days of the birth.

If the dependent child is born or adopted from the first through the 15th of the month, your group is billed for the rate change — if applicable — for the entire month in which the child is born or adopted. If the dependent child is born or adopted from the 16th through the last day of the month, your group is billed for the rate change — if applicable — starting the first of the following month in which the child is born or adopted.

Death of a member

In the event of a death, terminating a contract or removing a spouse/dependent becomes effective the first billing date for your group after the date of death. However, there are certain exceptions. For example, if your group reports a deceased subscriber who has active dependents remaining on the contract, the retroactive period is limited to 60 days.

Premiums for individual coverage

As the employer, you cannot pay the cost of individual health insurance premiums for your active employees. This may violate several laws, including but not limited to, the Employee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA), Medicare Secondary Payer (MSP), Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (PPACA).

We will reject an individual policy payment for an active employee if we know or have reason to believe the payment will violate a federal or state law, statute, regulation, or rule.

Healthcare reform and billing

You may see some changes to your invoice statement as a result of healthcare reform. Each covered employee will now be listed with their specific rate calculated on the monthly premium statement, so your invoice may become longer. Also, adding or removing a dependent will immediately impact the subscriber’s total rate on your group’s next monthly bill.
